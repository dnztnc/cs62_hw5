In one normal gameplay loop of Wordle, what is the bottleneck 
(the method in your implementation that takes the longest to run)? 
What is its run time, and as a function of what? (For example, if you say 
O(n), what is n?)

The big bottleneck is checking whether our word is in the list of 
valid words. For each input, this requires us to iterate over the 
entire list of valid words to compare our input, which takes up to 
O(n) time where n is the number of words in the list of valid words.

Can you think of any ways to speed it up? 
Describe a brief sketch of what your code 
could do to remove this bottleneck.

Use a set-like data structure (that is hashed), kind of like set() 
in Python. This would ensure O(1) time to check whether our input is
valid word, regardless of how big the list of valid words is.
